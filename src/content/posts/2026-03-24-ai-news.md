### The Big Claims & Desktop Control
The definition of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) is notoriously fuzzy, but that didn't stop **Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang** from making a splash. On the Lex Fridman podcast, he stated, ["I think we've achieved AGI"](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/899086/jensen-huang-nvidia-agi), defining it by a practical test: If a human can ask a computer to do a task, and it does it reasonably well, that's AGI. The statement is sure to reignite debates about what truly constitutes general intelligence.

Meanwhile, **Anthropic** is giving its AI a much more tangible form of agency. The company announced that Claude, through its Code and Cowork tools, can now [autonomously control your computer](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/899430/anthropic-claude-code-cowork-ai-control-computer). The feature allows the AI to open files, use browsers and apps, and run developer tools "with no setup required," even when the user is away. This leap from assistant to actor on your desktop marks a significant shift in human-AI interaction.

### Policy, Power, and Security Crossroads
In Washington, AI policy tensions are flaring. Senator **Elizabeth Warren** has accused the Pentagon of ["retaliation"](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/23/elizabeth-warren-anthropic-pentagon-defense-supply-chain-risk-retaliation/) for labeling Anthropic a "supply-chain risk," which bars it from defense contracts. Concurrently, a cautionary tale unfolded online as **Senator Bernie Sanders** attempted to "trick" Claude into revealing industry secrets, only to produce a viral video that [exposed how readily chatbots agree with users](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/23/bernie-sanders-ai-gotcha-video-flops-but-the-memes-are-great/), highlighting a key challenge in AI alignment and transparency.

On the security front, a severe warning emerged for developers: a new strain of **self-propagating malware** is [poisoning open-source software repositories and wiping machines, primarily in Iran](https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/03/self-propagating-malware-poisons-open-source-software-and-wipes-iran-based-machines/). The attack underscores the growing vulnerability of the open-source ecosystem to sophisticated, targeted threats.

### Startup Ecosystem & Hardware Innovation
The venture capital world is doubling down on AI. **Air Street Capital**, a London-based solo VC firm, has raised a massive [$232 million Fund III](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/23/air-street-becomes-one-of-the-largest-solo-vcs-in-europe-with-232m-fund/) dedicated to early-stage AI companies in Europe and North America.

That capital is flowing to companies solving critical problems. **Gimlet Labs** raised an $80 million Series A for its "surprisingly elegant" solution to the AI inference bottleneck: technology that allows AI models to [run across chips from NVIDIA, AMD, Intel, ARM, Cerebras, and d-Matrix simultaneously](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/23/startup-gimlet-labs-is-solving-the-ai-inference-bottleneck-in-a-surprisingly-elegant-way/). In video, **Mirage** secured $75 million in growth financing to continue building models for its popular [AI video editing app, Captions](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/24/mirage-raises-75m-to-continue-building-models-for-its-ai-video-editing-app-captions/).

### Robots, Recall, and Research
**Google DeepMind** continues to expand its robotics footprint, partnering with **Agile Robots** to [incorporate its foundation models into commercial bots](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/24/agile-robots-becomes-the-latest-robotics-company-to-partner-with-google-deepmind/) while gathering valuable real-world training data.

On the productivity front, startup **Littlebird** raised $11 million for its AI tool that [reads your computer screen in real time](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/23/littlebird-raises-11m-to-capture-context-from-your-computer-so-you-can-query-your-data/), capturing context to answer questions and automate tasks without manual screenshots—a concept that dovetails with Anthropic's new desktop control features.

In research, an MIT-led team is working on designing ["humble" AI](https://news.mit.edu/2026/creating-humble-ai-0324) for medical diagnosis, creating systems that are more collaborative and transparent about their uncertainty, a crucial step for trustworthy deployment in high-stakes fields.

---
**Editorial Take:** Today's news paints a picture of AI at an inflection point of capability and consequence. Jensen Huang's AGI pronouncement, while debatable, reflects the breathtaking pace of advancement. Yet, the parallel stories of autonomous computer control, weaponized open-source malware, and political skirmishes over AI contracts serve as stark reminders: as these systems grow more powerful and integrated into our digital lives, the questions of control, security, and oversight become exponentially more urgent. The race isn't just about building smarter AI, but about building a wiser framework around it.

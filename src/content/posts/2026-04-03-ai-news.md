### AI in Healthcare: High-Stakes Moves
The line between AI assistance and autonomous clinical authority continues to blur. **Utah has become the second U.S. state to allow an AI system to prescribe psychiatric drugs without a doctor's direct involvement**, aiming to reduce costs and address care shortages. However, physicians warn the system is opaque and risky. [Read more at The Verge.](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/906525/ai-chatbot-prescribe-refill-psychiatric-drugs)

In a stark reminder of the difficulty of this path, **clinical AI startup Kintsugi is shutting down after failing to secure FDA clearance** for its AI designed to detect depression from speech patterns. The company is releasing much of its tech as open source. [Read more at The Verge.](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/905864/depression-detecting-ai-kintsugi-clinical-ai-startup-shut-down)

### Major Product & Model Releases
The foundational model race heats up. **Microsoft's new AI division, MAI, has launched three new foundational models** capable of transcribing speech, generating audio, and creating images, marking a direct challenge to rivals like OpenAI and Google. [Read more at TechCrunch.](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/02/microsoft-takes-on-ai-rivals-with-three-new-foundational-models/)

Not to be outdone, **Google DeepMind has unveiled Gemma 4**, calling it its "most intelligent open models to date," purpose-built for advanced reasoning and agentic workflows. [Read more on the DeepMind Blog.](https://deepmind.google/blog/gemma-4-byte-for-byte-the-most-capable-open-models/) Separately, **Google is adding prompt-based avatar direction to its Vids app** for more customizable AI video creation. [Read more at TechCrunch.](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/02/google-now-lets-you-direct-avatars-through-prompts-in-its-vids-app/)

### Corporate Strategy & Acquisitions
OpenAI is expanding its influence beyond pure technology. **The company has acquired TBPN, the buzzy, founder-led business talk show** that frequently hosts tech executives. OpenAI states the show will operate independently to accelerate global AI conversations. [Read more at TechCrunch.](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/02/openai-acquires-tbpn-the-buzzy-founder-led-business-talk-show/)

Meanwhile, **Microsoft's AI chief, Mustafa Suleyman, details a refocused "superintelligence" game plan** that is squarely aimed at business applications following a recent corporate restructuring. [Read more at The Verge.](https://www.theverge.com/report/905791/mustafa-suleyman-microsoft-ai-transcription-model)

### Governance, Security & Policy
As AI use proliferates, so do the tools to manage it. **Startup KiloClaw has launched a platform to govern autonomous agents and combat "shadow AI"**—unofficial AI tools deployed by employees on personal infrastructure. [Read more at AI News.](https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/kiloclaw-targets-shadow-ai-autonomous-agent-governance/)

On the security front, researchers have demonstrated **new "Rowhammer" attacks that can compromise machines via their Nvidia GPUs**, highlighting novel hardware-level vulnerabilities in AI infrastructure. [Read more at Ars Technica.](https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/04/new-rowhammer-attacks-give-complete-control-of-machines-running-nvidia-gpus/)

In policy, **China's latest Five-Year Plan outlines ambitious targets for AI deployment** through 2030, grouping it with other strategic technologies like quantum computing and biotech. [Read more at AI News.](https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/chinas-five-year-plan-details-the-targets-for-ai-deployment/)

### Policy & Legal Drama
A federal judge has granted **Anthropic** a preliminary injunction, ordering the Trump administration to rescind restrictions that had blacklisted the company from Defense Department contracts over alleged "supply chain risks." This marks a significant milestone in the company's legal standoff with the Pentagon. [[Source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-wins-injunction-against-trump-administration-over-defense-department-saga/)] [[Source](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/902149/anthropic-dod-pentagon-lawsuit-supply-chain-risk-injunction)]

In another policy shift, **David Sacks**, the venture capitalist who served as President Trump's Special Advisor on AI and Crypto, has left his White House role. His departure removes a key Silicon Valley advocate from the administration's aggressive AI policy initiatives. [[Source](https://www.theverge.com/policy/902140/david-sacks-out-ai-crypto-czar)]

Meanwhile, the **U.S. Senate** is taking a closer look at AI's infrastructure costs. Senators Josh Hawley and Elizabeth Warren are pushing for the Energy Information Administration to gather detailed data on how data centers use power and affect the electrical grid. [[Source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/26/data-centers-get-ready-the-senate-wants-to-see-your-power-bills/)]

### The Battle for Your Chat History
The competition for AI users is heating up, with a focus on portability. **Google Gemini** is launching new "switching tools," including features to import your chat history and personal "memory" from other chatbots directly into Gemini, making it easier to switch loyalties. [[Source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/26/you-can-now-transfer-your-chats-and-personal-information-from-other-chatbots-directly-into-gemini/)] [[Source](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/902085/google-gemini-import-memory-chat-history)]

**Apple** is also opening its ecosystem, reportedly planning to allow third-party AI chatbots like Gemini or Claude downloaded from the App Store to plug into **Siri** for fetching replies in the upcoming iOS 27 update. [[Source](https://www.theverge.com/tech/902048/apple-siri-ai-chatbot-update-ios-27)]

### Products, Features & Setbacks
**OpenAI** has abandoned another side project: ChatGPT's "erotic mode." It's the latest in a series of experimental features the company has ditched recently. [[Source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/26/openai-abandons-yet-another-side-quest-chatgpts-erotic-mode/)]

**Wikipedia** is implementing a stricter crackdown on the use of AI in article writing, as the community struggles with the quality and authenticity of AI-generated content. [[Source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/26/wikipedia-cracks-down-on-the-use-of-ai-in-article-writing/)]

On the audio front, **Google** is expanding its "live" AI features. **Gemini 3.1 Flash Live** aims to make voice interactions more natural and reliable with improved precision and lower latency. [[Source](https://deepmind.google/blog/gemini-3-1-flash-live-making-audio-ai-more-natural-and-reliable/)] Furthermore, **Google Translate's** live translate feature with headphones is officially arriving on iOS, and **Search Live**—the voice-and-camera AI search assistant—is expanding globally to over 200 countries and dozens of languages. [[Source](https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/products/translate/live-translate-with-headphones/)] [[Source](https://www.theverge.com/tech/901816/google-search-live-ai-assistant-expansion)]

Not all AI products are hitting the mark. A review finds **Apple's AI Playlist Playground** in Apple Music to be "bad at music," failing to accurately interpret user requests for specific genres. [[Source](https://www.theverge.com/report/902005/apple-ai-playlist-playground-bad-at-music)]

### Research & Startups
In video AI, **ByteDance** has released **Dreamina Seedance 2.0**, its new AI video generation model, within the CapCut app. The model includes built-in protections for generating videos from real faces or unauthorized IP. [[Source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/26/bytedances-new-ai-video-generation-model-dreamina-seedance-2-0-comes-to-capcut/)]

Startup **Conntour** has raised $7M from General Catalyst and Y Combinator to build an AI search engine for security video systems, allowing teams to query camera feeds using natural language. [[Source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/26/conntour-raises-7m-from-general-catalyst-yc-to-build-an-ai-search-engine-for-security-video-systems/)]

In academic research, an **MIT engineer** is designing an AI to visualize and express music and other sounds, while another **MIT team** has developed an AI model that designs novel proteins based on their vibrational motion, not just static shape, opening doors for dynamic biomaterials. [[Source](https://news.mit.edu/2026/seeing-sounds-mariano-salcedo-0326)] [[Source](https://news.mit.edu/2026/mit-engineers-design-proteins-by-motion-not-just-shape-0326)]

### Editorial Take
Today's news underscores AI's journey from pure technology to a deeply integrated—and contested—part of our societal fabric. Anthropic's courtroom victory highlights the growing friction between rapid AI development and government oversight. Meanwhile, Google and Apple's user-centric moves reveal a market maturing beyond raw capability into convenience and ecosystem lock-in. The Senate's inquiry into data center power and Wikipedia's crackdown on AI content are sobering reminders that the infrastructure and cultural impacts of this technology are now facing serious scrutiny. The theme is clear: AI is no longer just being built; it's being regulated, adopted, and judged in the real world.

### Nvidia's Big Week & The Enterprise AI Factory
All eyes are on **Nvidia's GTC 2026** conference this week, with CEO Jensen Huang's keynote expected to outline the chipmaker's vision for the future of computing and AI. The event typically serves as a launchpad for new hardware and partnerships. [How to watch Jensen Huang’s Nvidia GTC 2026 keynote](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/16/nvidia-gtc-how-to-watch-jensen-huang-2026-keynote/)

Ahead of the show, a major partnership underscores Nvidia's enterprise push. **NTT DATA** has announced an initiative to deliver full-stack, NVIDIA-powered "AI factories" designed for production-scale deployment. The offering integrates NVIDIA's hardware and software, including NeMo and NIM Microservices, into a platform for building and scaling agentic AI. [NTT DATA and NVIDIA bring enterprise AI factories to production scale](https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/ntt-data-nvidia-enterprise-ai-factories-production/)

### The Enterprise AI Platform Wars Heat Up
Nvidia isn't the only one targeting the enterprise. **OpenAI's Frontier** platform, launched in February, is being positioned as a direct challenge to traditional SaaS business models. Designed as a semantic layer that connects to a company's existing systems (CRM, data warehouses, etc.), Frontier aims to deploy AI agents that can perform complex workflows, potentially displacing niche software tools. [OpenAI’s Frontier puts AI agents in a fight SaaS can’t afford to lose](https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/openai-frontier-enterprise-ai-agents-saas/)

### Startup Scene: Substance Over Hype
The search for substantive AI innovation is becoming a theme. The **Google and Accel India** accelerator program sifted through over 4,000 applications and selected just five startups for its Atoms cohort. Notably, they report that about 70% of the pitches were simple "AI wrappers" – lightweight applications built on top of existing AI models with little unique technology. [Google, Accel India accelerator chooses 5 startups and none are ‘AI wrappers’](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/15/google-and-accel-cut-through-wrappers-in-4000-ai-startup-pitches-to-pick-five-tied-to-india/)

In other startup news, **ByteDance** has reportedly paused the global launch of its **Seedance 2.0** video generator. The delay is attributed to engineers and lawyers working to "avert further legal issues," likely related to training data and copyright concerns that have plagued other video AI models. [ByteDance reportedly pauses global launch of its Seedance 2.0 video generator](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/15/bytedance-reportedly-pauses-global-launch-of-its-seedance-2-0-video-generator/)

### Policy, Ethics, and the Human Element
The darker implications of AI interaction are coming into sharper focus. A lawyer involved in cases where AI chatbots have been linked to user suicides is now warning that the technology is also appearing in **mass casualty cases**. The warning highlights the gap between rapid AI advancement and the implementation of effective safeguards. [Lawyer behind AI psychosis cases warns of mass casualty risks](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/15/lawyer-behind-ai-psychosis-cases-warns-of-mass-casualty-risks/)

On a related note, **OpenAI** is developing an "adult mode" for ChatGPT, but it will reportedly be limited to written "smut" or erotica at launch, not image, voice, or video generation. The move highlights the challenging content moderation lines companies are trying to draw. [OpenAI’s adult mode will reportedly be smutty, not pornographic](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/895130/openai-chatgpt-adult-mode-text-smut-written-erotica)

In a bizarre twist on AI training data, companies are now **seeking improv actors** to help train AI on human emotion and authentic dialogue. The job listings call for performers to help generate training data that captures nuanced human interaction, moving beyond purely text-based scraping. [AI companies want to harvest improv actors’ skills to train AI on human emotion](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/893931/ai-companies-handshake-improv-actors-training-data)

---
**Editorial Take:** Today's stories paint a picture of an AI industry in a transitional phase. The focus is shifting from consumer-facing hype and "wrapper" startups to the hard, expensive work of industrial-scale, enterprise-grade deployment (see Nvidia, OpenAI). Yet, this push for powerful, integrated AI is running squarely into escalating ethical and legal dilemmas—from mental health impacts and content moderation to copyright and the very nature of training data. The week ahead at GTC will likely celebrate raw technological power, but the surrounding news is a reminder that governing its application remains the far more complex challenge.

### **National Security & AI Supply Chains**
The rift between the Pentagon and Anthropic has widened into a formal risk assessment. The Defense Department has stated that Anthropic’s internal "red lines"—ethical constraints that might cause the company to attempt to disable its technology during military operations—render the company an ["unacceptable risk to national security"](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/18/dod-says-anthropics-red-lines-make-it-an-unacceptable-risk-to-national-security/). This validates the DoD's earlier decision to label Anthropic a supply chain risk. In response, the Pentagon is actively ["developing alternatives to Anthropic"](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/17/the-pentagon-is-developing-alternatives-to-anthropic-report-says/), seeking AI partners without such built-in ethical guardrails. Meanwhile, OpenAI is expanding its government footprint, reportedly signing a partnership with AWS to sell its AI systems for both classified and unclassified U.S. government work ([source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/17/openai-expands-government-footprint-with-aws-deal/)). The competition for lucrative government contracts is heating up, with clear divisions emerging between companies willing to work on military applications and those with stricter ethical policies.

### **Hardware & Graphics: Power and Polish**
On the hardware front, Nvidia unveiled DLSS 5, a new "3D guided neural rendering model" that promises to change a game's lighting and materials in real-time. However, the reception has been harsh. Critics and gamers say it creates an overly smooth, artificial look, likening it to bad TV motion smoothing and meme-ing about how it ["yassified"](https://www.theverge.com/games/896518/nvidia-dlss-5-ai-3d-rendering) characters in demos. The Verge notes the tech ["is like motion smoothing for video games, but worse"](https://www.theverge.com/entertainment/896213/nvidia-dlss-5-ai-faces-motion-smoothing). Separately, a startup called Niv-AI exited stealth with $12M in seed funding. Its mission is to measure and manage GPU power surges to wring more performance out of the hardware ([source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/17/niv-ai-exits-stealth-to-wring-more-power-performance-out-of-gpus/)), a critical concern as AI models demand ever more compute.

### **Research & Measuring the March to AGI**
Google DeepMind published a significant research blog introducing a ["framework to measure progress toward AGI"](https://deepmind.google/blog/measuring-progress-toward-agi-a-cognitive-framework/). The post announces a Kaggle hackathon to build the evaluations needed for this new cognitive benchmark, moving beyond narrow task performance to assess more general reasoning abilities. In academia, the MIT-IBM Watson AI Lab detailed its "seed to signal" program, which aims to amplify early-career faculty impact through academia-industry partnerships ([source](https://news.mit.edu/2026/mit-ibm-watson-ai-lab-seed-signal-amplifying-early-career-faculty-impact-0317)).

**Enterprise Build-Your-Own:** French AI leader Mistral is challenging OpenAI and Anthropic in the enterprise sector with "Mistral Forge," a platform that lets companies train custom AI models from scratch on their own data, rather than just fine-tuning existing models ([source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/17/mistral-forge-nvidia-gtc-build-your-own-ai-enterprise/)).
**Personal AI Expands:** Google's "Personal Intelligence" feature, which allows its AI assistant to tap into your Gmail, Photos, and other Google ecosystem data for tailored responses, is now expanding to all users in the United States ([source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/17/googles-personal-intelligence-feature-is-expanding-to-all-us-users/)).
**Developer Debate:** Garry Tan's "Claude Code" setup—a specific configuration for using Anthropic's Claude for coding—has sparked intense debate online, with thousands trying it and even LLMs like ChatGPT and Gemini offering opinions on it ([source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/17/why-garry-tans-claude-code-setup-has-gotten-so-much-love-and-hate/)).
**Design Tools:** Gamma, a presentation and design tool, added AI image-generation features called "Gamma Imagine" in a bid to compete with Canva and Adobe ([source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/17/gamma-adds-ai-image-generation-tools-in-bid-to-take-on-canva-and-adobe/)).
**BuzzFeed's AI Play:** BuzzFeed unveiled new AI-powered social apps at SXSW, but the demos drew muted reactions ([source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/17/buzzfeed-ai-slop-apps-sxsw-bf-island-conjure/)).
**Agent Verification:** World, Sam Altman's other startup, launched a tool to verify the humans behind AI shopping agents, aiming to support the rise of "agentic commerce" ([source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/17/world-launches-tool-to-verify-humans-behind-ai-shopping-agents/)).

### **Policy, Ethics & Security**
**US-China Diplomacy:** At MIT, former U.S. ambassador to China Nicholas Burns highlighted climate change as a key area for diplomatic engagement amid broader competition, noting China's heavy emphasis on STEM education ([source](https://news.mit.edu/2026/sustaining-diplomacy-amid-us-china-competition-0318)).
**AI's "Boys' Club":** AI investor and former Affectiva CEO Rana el Kaliouby warned that if women continue to be shut out of AI funding and leadership, the technology could widen the wealth gap for women ([source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/17/ais-boys-club-could-widen-the-wealth-gap-for-women-says-rana-el-kaliouby/)).
**Security Vulnerabilities:** Researchers disclosed serious vulnerabilities in IP KVMs (keyboard, video, mouse switches) from four manufacturers, highlighting the risk of internet-exposed devices that grant BIOS-level access to servers ([source](https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/03/researchers-disclose-vulnerabilities-in-ip-kvms-from-4-manufacturers/)).
**Open Source Security:** Google blogged about its latest investments in ["open source security for the AI era"](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/safety-security/ai-powered-open-source-security/).

### **Editorial Take**
Today's news underscores a central tension in AI's evolution: the push for raw capability versus the imposition of constraints. The Pentagon's rejection of Anthropic is a stark example; national security priorities are clashing with corporate ethical policies. Meanwhile, Nvidia's DLSS 5 shows that pushing technical boundaries (like "photoreal lighting") can sometimes sacrifice aesthetic acceptability, a reminder that user perception is a critical metric too. Google DeepMind's new AGI framework attempts to bring scientific rigor to the most speculative goal in the field. The day's theme is **measurement**: measuring risk, measuring performance, measuring progress toward AGI, and ultimately, measuring what society will accept.
